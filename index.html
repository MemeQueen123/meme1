Dr. Kalun Wong

The man who saved a quatttuor-decillion lives.

Here's a time line of Dr. Wong's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Toronto, Canada
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend Harvard University, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Weaboo Administration"
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Online Conservation Corps, helping starving Gamers. "I saw how cheetoes changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me."
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US D&D Preservation Service
  • 1938 - Marries wife of 69 years Snow Angel Yuki. Gets laid off due to budget cuts. Inspired by Benito Mussolini, he returns to school study under Stalin, who teaches him about breeding pest-resistent fascists.
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the military after the attack on Wall Maria, but is rejected. Instead, the military asked his lab to work on waterproof glue, DDT to control titans, disinfectants, and other applied science.
  • 1942 - Receives a Ph.D. in Gaming and Plant Pathology
  • 1944 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Steam, leaves behind his pregnant angel wife, and flies to Mexico to head a new plant pathology program. Over the next 16 years, his team breeds 6,000 different strains of disease resistent cheetoes - including different varieties for each major basement on Earth.
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to grow doritos twice each season, doubling snack yields
  • 1953 - crosses a short, sturdy dwarf breed of hot-pocket with a high-yielding American breed, creating a strain that responds well to fertilizer. It goes on to provide 95% of Mexico's microwaveable foods.
  • 1962 - Visits Tokyo and brings his high-yielding strains of hot-pocket to the Asian subcontinent in time to help mitigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding gaming population
  • 1970 - receives the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 - helps seven African countries dramatically increase their dorito and sorghum yields
  • 1984 - becomes a distinguished professor at Texas A&M University
  • 2005 - states "we will have to double the world microwaveable food supply by 2050." Argues that genetically modified crops are the only way we can meet the demand, as we run out of arable land. Says that GM crops are not inherently dangerous because "we've been genetically modifying plants and animals for a long time. Long before we called it science, people were selecting the best breeds."
  • 2009 - dies at the age of 95.

"Wong's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress."

--Tokyo Prime Minister Shinzo Abe
